🍝 Feral Spaghetti — Cook It Right
Two ways. Infinite options. No wrong answers.
The concept:
Noodles. Vegetables. Something to coat them with. That's it. Everything else is up to you.
What you need:
Noodles — whatever kind you have
Vegetables — whatever you have or whatever was cheap this week
Option 1: tomato sauce
Option 2: butter and/or olive oil + salt + pepper
Meat — optional, genuinely optional
How to make it:
Cook your noodles. Cook your vegetables. Combine them. Eat.
The important part:
Cook your vegetables until they work for YOUR body. Some people need them with a little bite still in them. Some people need them basically melted. Both are correct. Neither is wrong.
I was diagnosed with gastroparesis which means my stomach has a hard time digesting food properly and fiber is especially hard. I have to cook my vegetables way past what most recipes call for so my stomach can actually handle them. If you have gastroparesis, IBS, Crohn's, sensory issues, or just prefer softer textures overcook those veggies. If you like them crunchy and your body can handle it go for it. However you want them totally works. There is no vegetable police. Don't let anyone yuck your yum. 😏 eyebrows eyebrows 🤭
The kid trick:
Let them pick the vegetables. Broccoli, carrots, cauliflower, green beans, edamame, asparagus — whatever they choose goes in. Kids eat what they helped choose. Every time.
The budget part:
Buy whatever vegetables are cheapest or on sale that week. This recipe works with literally all of them.
Side note: cooking time actually changes the nutritional value of food. Sometimes overcooking releases more nutrients. Sometimes undercooking preserves them. It depends on the vegetable and what your body needs. We'll link the research here so you can nerd out on it yourself.
And if you do your own research and find something that contradicts anything in here we genuinely want to know about it. This app is only as good as the information in it and we would rather be corrected than wrong. Same goes for your own experience. I was diagnosed with gastroparesis and I cannot eat a salad without wanting to cry. I know someone else with the exact same diagnosis who eats salads just fine but can't touch french fries. Same condition. Completely different bodies. You are the expert on yours. DM us if something in here doesn't match your experience. We'll figure it out together.

Someone Should Have Told You — Frozen Vegetables
The big bag of frozen broccoli that costs $1.50 and lasts your family multiple meals? Science says it's just as good as the fresh crown — and if that fresh crown sat in your fridge for five days before you cooked it, the frozen bag actually won.
Here's why: vegetables destined for freezing are harvested at peak ripeness and processed within hours, locking in vitamins and minerals at their highest point. Fresh vegetables spend days or weeks in transit and on store shelves losing nutrients the entire time. If you're eating fresh produce within a day or two of buying it you get a slight edge. If it sits in your fridge longer than that, frozen is likely the better source of vitamins.
Frozen corn, blueberries, and green beans have actually been found to have MORE vitamin C than their fresh equivalents.
Stop feeling bad about buying frozen. You made the right call.
